We Must STOP Bullying

What is Bullying?

Bullying is the treatment toward another individual that is generally aggressive, repeated over time, and represents an imbalance of real or perceived power. It is important to recognize that while bullying is mostly associated with children, it is not exclusive to them.

Forms of Bullying Verbal
  • Teasing
  • Name-calling
  • Taunting
  • Physical Threats
Social
  • Spreading rumors
  • Public embarassment
  • Excluding an individual
  • Cyberbullying
Physical
  • Hitting/Kicking
  • Tripping/Pushing
  • Taking or breaking property
Affects of Bullying

Individuals who are bullied may have negative impacts in many aspects in life such as

  • Physical Health
  • Mental and Emotional Health
  • Academic Standing
  • Social Standing

These effects may cause trauma and affect those continuing into adulthood

Prevention

Teaching children what bullying is and how to confront it safely can help them. It is important to educate them on different ways to get help.
Keeping a safe and open communication with your children will help them be more comfortable bringing up rather uncomfortable situations/conversations. Keeping the constant communication can also help you as the parent, to noticing when something is off.
Being a role model to your children and showing them how to treat others respectfully and kindly will help them replicate those actions.
